## Lessons Learned

### What Worked Well
1. **Parallel research execution**: 4 agents completed comprehensive research in ~6 minutes
2. **Modern source prioritization**: Post-2000 scholarship consistently more accurate
3. **Balanced assessment framework**: Acknowledging successes AND failures
4. **Declassified evidence focus**: Venona, Nuremberg, archives trump older sources
5. **Ethical frameworks**: Raeder profile demonstrates separating competence from crimes

### What This Reveals About Historical Profiles
1. **Propaganda persistence**: Wartime/Cold War narratives survive in modern profiles
2. **Fabricated data**: Numbers cited without sources (Plan Z, troop strength)
3. **Sanitization**: Uncomfortable truths minimized or omitted (espionage, war crimes, PTSD abuse)
4. **Attribution errors**: Credit given to wrong individuals (Nagano/Yamamoto)
5. **Hero worship**: Allied commanders receive inflated credit for unit achievements

### Application to Future Profiles
1. **Always verify "firsts" claims** - often propaganda or technically incorrect
2. **Check post-declassification scholarship** - especially for Cold War subjects
3. **Distinguish personal vs unit achievements** - commanders get credit for subordinate work
4. **Verify numbers with primary sources** - inflated statistics common
5. **Address ethical issues honestly** - technical competence ‚â† moral absolution
