## Verification Methodology Assessment

### What Worked Exceptionally Well

1. **Independent verification mandate**: Verifiers successfully challenged assumptions with historical evidence
2. **Parallel execution**: 28 concurrent Haiku agents completed verification in ~30 minutes
3. **Critical thinking emphasis**: Agents analyzed evidence and corrected when contradicted, didn't rubber-stamp
4. **Haiku model selection**: Fast, cheap verification tasks well-suited to Haiku's capabilities
5. **Catch rate**: 20 of 28 profiles (71%) required corrections - verification prevented error propagation

### Critical Discoveries

1. **Propaganda Detection**: Groves "zero leaks" exposed as false narrative built on selective omission
2. **Sanitization Patterns**: Montgomery profile omitted major failure, Raeder profile whitewashed Nazi collaboration
3. **Attribution Errors**: Nagano "Architect", Patton "first Rhine crossing", Galland "Schwarm" all misattributed
4. **Mythology vs Reality**: Hopper COBOL role perpetuates popular myth contradicted by primary sources
5. **Numerical Inflation**: Patton prisoners, MacArthur personnel, Raeder Z-Plan all overstated

### Patterns Identified

**Common Error Types:**
1. **Sanitization** (omitting failures): Montgomery, Groves, Raeder
2. **Attribution errors** (crediting wrong person): Nagano, Galland, Hopper
3. **Numerical inflation** (overstating achievements): Patton, MacArthur, Raeder
4. **Minor date errors** (off by days/months): King, Eisenhower, Nimitz
5. **Role conflation** (coordinator vs executor): Nagano, Layton, Bedell Smith

**Red Flags for Future Verification:**
- Superlatives without qualification ("first", "greatest", "zero")
- Round numbers (1M prisoners, 350km, 90% reduction)
- Omission of well-documented failures
- Attribution without specific sources
- Post-war mythology vs wartime reality gaps

## Lessons Learned

### What Worked Exceptionally Well

1. **Haiku Model Selection**: Fast, cheap, effective for verification tasks
2. **Parallel Execution**: 28 concurrent agents completed in ~30 minutes vs 14 hours sequential
3. **Critical Thinking Mandate**: "Verify and challenge" produced 71% correction rate
4. **Detailed Reporting**: Specific findings with evidence enable targeted corrections
5. **Team Diversity**: 28 specialized verifiers covered breadth of military history

### What Could Improve

1. **Verification Checklist**: Standard checklist could ensure consistency (dates, numbers, attributions, omissions)
2. **Source Requirements**: Profiles should include inline citations for major claims
3. **Peer Review**: High-stakes claims (superlatives, firsts) should require second verification
4. **Mythology Database**: Common myths (Hopper COBOL, Groves zero leaks) should be documented
5. **Failure Documentation**: Explicit requirement to document major failures, not just successes

### Critical Insights

1. **Verification Prevents Compounding**: Errors in template profiles (Nimitz) would propagate to derivatives
2. **Sanitization is Subtle**: Omissions (Montgomery Market Garden) harder to detect than false claims
3. **Attribution is Complex**: Coordinator/approver/advisor often conflated with designer/creator/planner
4. **Mythology Persists**: Popular narratives (Hopper COBOL, Groves security) resist correction
5. **Numerical Inflation Common**: Round numbers often approximations presented as facts
