## Campaign-Level Lessons

### What Worked

**1. Meticulous Upfront Planning**
- Montgomery's batch division by specialty prevented duplicate work
- Clear task assignments with specific commander rosters
- Template established before parallel work began

**2. Parallel Execution**
- 4 design teams working simultaneously (9-10 cards each)
- Zero coordination overhead once batches assigned
- Total time: ~2 hours vs sequential estimate of 6-8 hours

**3. Quality Gates**
- Ramsay validation at end caught zero defects
- Proves upfront template + clear specs > post-hoc verification
- Quality baked in, not inspected in

**4. Multi-Source Strategy**
- Photo acquisition tried fastest approach first (SearXNG)
- Fell back to systematic delays when rate limited
- Final 2 photos completed via alternative sources (DVIDS, Wikimedia manual)

**5. Proper Escalation**
- Nimitz correctly identified when automation exhausted
- Didn't waste time retrying failed approaches
- Clear communication enabled Field Marshal to complete final 2 photos

### What Didn't Work

**1. Photo Acquisition via Automated Wikimedia**
- Rate limiting more aggressive than anticipated (~40 downloads vs expected unlimited)
- Automated curl/wget blocked regardless of headers or delays
- Lesson: Government sites (archives.gov, DVIDS) more automation-friendly

**2. Initial Photo Count Estimate**
- Estimated 30 missing photos, actual 32
- Minor issue but shows need for reconnaissance before planning
- Lesson: Always verify actual count before batch sizing

**2. ImageMagick Pipeline**

**3. Batched Commits**
- Progress visibility during long-running tasks
- Easy rollback if issues discovered
- Clear audit trail of work completed

## Recommendations for Future Campaigns

### Process Improvements

1. **Photo Acquisition Separate from SVG Creation**
   - Different skillsets (design vs systematic downloading)
   - Parallel execution: one team creates SVGs, another acquires photos
   - Photos can lag SVG creation without blocking

2. **Reconnaissance Phase**
   - Verify actual counts before batch planning
   - Test external dependencies (Wikimedia, archives.gov) before relying on them
   - Document rate limits and access patterns

3. **Progressive Commits**
   - Commit work in batches (every 10 cards, every 10 photos)
   - Enables progress tracking and early issue detection
   - Reduces risk of large-scale rework

### Commander Assignments

**Best Fits Validated**:
- Montgomery: Supreme coordination (proven again)
- Nimitz: Systematic multi-phase operations (photo acquisition perfect fit)
- Ramsay: Quality enforcement (zero tolerance prevented time waste)

**New Insights**:
- Bradley/Zhukov: First AI deployments, both delivered flawlessly (good baseline)
- King: Data visualization extends to SVG card design (broader than expected)

### Technical Architecture

**Keep**:
- Template-driven design (enabled parallel work)
- SVG format (crisp at any scale, embeddable, accessible)
- 100×100px photo standard (fast loading, consistent presentation)

**Improve**:
- Photo source priority: DVIDS/archives.gov > Wikimedia
- Automated validation: Ramsay's checks could be scripted for CI/CD
- Card variants: Consider creating multiple sizes (thumbnail, full, print)
