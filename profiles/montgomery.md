## World War II Achievements

Bernard Montgomery took command of the battered 8th Army in August 1942 and turned catastrophic retreat into overwhelming victory. He planned El Alamein — and won it before the first shot was fired.

**Career highlights:**
- Second Battle of El Alamein (October 1942): Decisive Allied victory in North Africa
- Operation Torch, Sicily, and Italian campaigns
- D-Day, Normandy (June 1944): Commanded all Allied ground forces for the initial invasion
- Battle of the Bulge response: Commanded northern flank
- German surrender (May 4, 1945): Accepted German surrender on Luneburg Heath

**Leadership style**: Meticulous planner. Would not fight unless certain of winning. Insufferable to peers, beloved by troops. Never improvised — every operation choreographed to the last detail.
