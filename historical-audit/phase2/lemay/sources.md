## Tier 1: Primary Sources Contradicting Self-Interest

### USSBS Pacific War Summary Report (1946)

**Access level**: PARTIAL (~60%)
**URL**: https://www.ibiblio.org/hyperwar/AAF/USSBS/PTO-Summary.html (HTML version, accessible)
**PDF version**: https://archive.org/download/unitedstatesstra00cent/unitedstatesstra00cent.pdf (BLOCKED — maxContentLength exceeded, file too large)
**Reliability tier**: Tier 1 (contemporary official US government document — self-critical in acknowledging limitations of bombing, authored by the victorious power but conducted as a genuine survey)

**Evidence extracted**:
- "Fifteen square miles of Tokyo's most densely populated area were burned to the ground."
- "No subsequent urban area attack was equally destructive."
- "approximately 185,000 were suffered in the initial attack on Tokyo of 9 March 1945" [total casualties: killed + injured]
- "Total civilian casualties in Japan, as a result of 9 months of air attack, including those from the atomic bombs, were approximately 806,000. Of these, approximately 330,000 were fatalities."
- "probably exceeded Japan's combat casualties which the Japanese estimate as having totaled approximately 780,000 during the entire war"
- Industrial output fell to "40 percent of the 1944 peak" by July 1945
- "certain of the United States commanders...stated their belief that, by the coordinated impact of blockade and direct air attack, Japan could be forced to surrender without invasion."
- "One thousand six hundred and sixty-seven tons of bombs were dropped on Tokyo in the first attack."

**Methodological assessment**: The USSBS was conducted by American military and civilian experts immediately after the war (1945-1946). It represents contemporaneous official assessment. However, it was conducted by the same military that conducted the bombing, creating conflict-of-interest. The survey's conclusions about Japan potentially surrendering without invasion/atomic bombs should be read as the USSBS attempting to validate its preferred conclusion (that bombing won the war independently). Multiple historians (including Frank's "Downfall") have challenged some USSBS conclusions as overreaching.

**Limitations**: Key Report 090 (Effects of Incendiary Bomb Attacks on Japan) not accessed in full — this would be the most directly relevant document for casualty analysis.

### USSBS — Japan's Struggle to End the War (1946)

**Access level**: PARTIAL (~40%)
**URL**: https://www.ibiblio.org/hyperwar/AAF/USSBS/JapansStruggle/ (HTML version, timeout on full load; key conclusions extracted via SearXNG search results)
**Reliability tier**: Tier 1 (same qualification as above)

**Evidence extracted**:
- "Japan would have surrendered even if the atomic bombs had not been dropped, even if Russia had not entered the war, and even if no invasion had been planned or contemplated."
- Atomic bombs "provided urgency and lubrication but changed no votes" on accepting surrender
- Soviet entry "neither defeated Japan nor materially hastened acceptance nor changed votes"
- Japan's collapse was driven by air superiority, naval blockade, and strategic bombing as an "accelerant"

**Critical note**: This conclusion is disputed by Richard Frank ("Downfall," 1999) and others who argue the USSBS overstated the certainty of Japanese surrender without atomic intervention. The USSBS conclusion represents one position in an ongoing historical debate, not settled fact.

**Limitations**: Full document not loaded due to timeout. Key conclusions extracted from academic citations via SearXNG. Full reading required for complete assessment.

**Search attempted**: SearXNG search for AFHRA documents, NARA catalog search
**Result**: These records exist but require institutional access or in-person AFHRA visit

**Why this matters**: The operational orders would document:
1. Official stated objectives of Operation Meetinghouse (industrial vs civilian targets)
2. Exact tactical parameters ordered (altitude, bomb type, target coordinates)
3. Who formally signed off on the specific March 9 raid

**Impact of gap**: Cannot definitively establish from primary source whether LeMay formally characterized the raid as targeting "industrial dispersal" or whether he acknowledged targeting civilian areas. ## Tier 5: Secondary Sources (General Historical, Pre-2000)

### Britannica Encyclopedia — "Bombing of Tokyo" article

**Access level**: PARTIAL (full article read via WebFetch)
**Reliability tier**: Tier 5 (editorial secondary source)

**Evidence extracted**:
- 334 B-29s launched; 279 dropped ordnance
- 1,665 tons of incendiary bombs including napalm and white phosphorus
- Conservative estimate: at least 80,000 killed; likely over 100,000
- Total from all firebombings: 300,000-330,000 Japanese civilians killed
- 67 of 71 major Japanese cities attacked
- "no distinction was made between industrial, military, and civilian targets"

**Reliability assessment**: Britannica is a reliable secondary source with editorial oversight. Figures consistent with USSBS and other sources. No primary citations provided in the accessible version.

**Reliability tier**: Tier 5 (institutional secondary source, curated)
