## Key Verification Questions

### 1. Tokyo Firebombing - Casualty Accounting

**Claims to verify**:
- "100,000 killed in one night" (commonly cited)
- "Most destructive bombing raid in history"
- "Military necessity"

**Evidence needed**:
- Japanese government casualty reports (primary source)
- US Strategic Bombing Survey findings
- Contemporary estimates vs post-war research
- Casualty breakdown: military vs civilian
- Comparison to other raids (Dresden, Hamburg)

**Statistical verification**:
- Square miles burned
- Population density of target area
- Incendiary bomb effectiveness
- Fire spread patterns
- Casualty estimation methodology

### 2. Ethics and Alternatives

**Claims to verify**:
- "No alternative to area bombing"
- "Japanese dispersed war production into cities"
- "Shortened the war, saved lives"

**Evidence needed**:
- Target selection records: Were industrial targets available?
- Precision bombing capability in 1945
- British experience: Precision vs area bombing effectiveness
- Japanese surrender factors: Bombing vs atomic bombs vs Soviet entry
- LeMay's justifications vs contemporary alternatives proposed

**Ethical assessment**:
- Intentional targeting of civilians vs collateral damage
- Low-altitude tactics (reduced accuracy, increased civilian deaths)
- Incendiary vs high-explosive choice
- International law context (Hague Conventions)

### 3. Decision-Making Authority

**Claims to verify**:
- "LeMay decided on low-altitude tactics himself"
- "Arnold approved the strategy"
- "Truman administration was informed"

**Evidence needed**:
- Chain of command documentation
- Who approved shift from high-altitude to low-altitude?
- Who approved shift from precision to area bombing?
- When did civilian leadership know about tactics?
- LeMay's authority vs requiring higher approval

### 4. Effectiveness Assessment

**Claims to verify**:
- "Strategic bombing shortened the war"
- "Destroyed Japanese war production"
- "Alternative to invasion"

**Evidence needed**:
- US Strategic Bombing Survey conclusions
- Japanese war production data (before vs after bombing)
- Japanese surrender decision factors (primary sources)
- Atomic bomb necessity vs conventional bombing alternative
- Soviet entry impact vs bombing impact

**Counterfactual analysis**:
- "Saved lives by avoiding invasion" - what evidence?
- "Alternative to atomic bombs" - contemporaneous consideration?
- Comparison to Germany strategic bombing effectiveness

### 5. Comparison to Arthur Harris (RAF)

**Both controversial for area bombing**:
- Harris (Dresden, Hamburg) vs LeMay (Tokyo)
- British vs American public acceptance
- Post-war treatment: Harris denied honors, LeMay became CSAF
- Double standard? Different contexts?

**Evidence needed**:
- Casualty comparisons (Dresden vs Tokyo)
- Tactical differences (British night bombing vs American daylight)
- Post-war accountability differences
