#### Ohlendorf Testimony (Nuremberg, January 3, 1946)

**Access**: FULL - Complete testimony retrieved and read
**Length**: ~15,000 words
**Source**: https://famous-trials.com/nuremberg/1934-ohlentestimony

**Key Evidence**:

1. **Wehrmacht-Einsatzgruppen cooperation**:
   > "The agreement specified that the army groups or armies would be responsible for the movement and the supply of Einsatzgruppen, but that instructions for their activities would come from the chief of Sipo and the SD." (lines 223-224)

2. **Army operational control**:
   > "There existed a general agreement that the army was also entitled to issue orders to the Einsatzgruppen if the operational situation made it necessary." (line 231)

3. **11th Army commanders identified**:
   > **COL. AMEN:** Who was the commanding officer of the 11th Army?
   > **OHLENDORF:** At first, Riter von Schober; later Von Manstein. (lines 290-291)

4. **11th Army direction of liquidations**:
   > "An order from the 11th Army was sent to Nikolaiev that liquidations were to take place only at a distance of not less than two hundred kilometeres from the headquarters of the commanding general." (lines 292-293)

5. **Simferopol acceleration order**:
   > "In Simferopol the army command requested the Einsatzkommandos in its area to hasten liquidations, because famine was threatening and there was a great housing shortage." (lines 294-295)

6. **Scale of operations**:
   > **COL. AMEN:** Do you know how many persons were liquidated by Einsatz Group D under your command?
   > **OHLENDORF:** In the year between June 1941 to June 1942 the Einsatzkommandos reported ninety thousand people liquidated. (lines 296-297)

7. **Wehrmacht knowledge of mission**:
   > "Himmler told me that before the beginning of the Russian campaign Hitler had spoken of this mission to a conference of the army groups and the army chiefs...and had instructed the commanding generals to provide the necessary support." (lines 270-272)

**Reliability**: HIGHEST - Testimony under oath, contradicts self-interest (Ohlendorf condemns himself), specific details, corroborated by other Einsatzgruppen commanders

### The Acquittal Problem: Why Historians Disagree

**Tribunal's reasoning** (from secondary sources):
1. High evidentiary bar: Required direct evidence of Manstein personally ordering specific Jewish massacres
2. Wehrmacht-Einsatzgruppen cooperation: Army provided logistics, but Einsatzgruppen operated under SS command
3. November 20, 1941 order: Interpreted as "anti-partisan" not "anti-Jewish" (despite explicit text)
4. Ohlendorf testimony: Simferopol order attributed to "army command," not specifically Manstein
5. Soviet sources: Potentially politically influenced (1949 Cold War suspicions)

**Modern scholarship consensus** (post-1990s):
1. Evidentiary bar was SET TOO HIGH: November 20 order IS direct evidence of antisemitic intent
2. "Insufficient linkage" is LEGALLY defensible but HISTORICALLY absurd: 11th Army directed operations, Manstein signed order targeting Jews, 90,000 killed in his operational area
3. Cold War politics INFLUENCED verdict: Western establishment needed Wehrmacht generals for NATO planning, West German rearmament
4. Tribunal made ARTIFICIAL distinction: Convicted on "military" war crimes (commissars, POWs), acquitted on "ideological" crimes (Holocaust) - but November 20 order proves ideological motivation

**Conclusion**: Hamburg acquittal on Holocaust charges is LEGALLY valid (tribunal had authority to set evidentiary standards) but HISTORICALLY indefensible (evidence was overwhelming).
