## Subject Overview

**Full Name**: Fritz Erich Georg Eduard von Lewinski, called von Manstein
**Service**: Wehrmacht (German Army), 1906-1944
**Highest Rank**: Generalfeldmarschall (Field Marshal)
**Notable Commands**:
- Chief of Staff, Army Group South (1939-40)
- Commander, 11th Army (Crimea, 1941-42)
- Commander, Army Group Don/South (Stalingrad relief, 1942-44)

**Known For**:
- Fall Gelb (1940 France campaign) operational plan - "Manstein Plan"
- Conquest of Sevastopol (1942)
- Failed Stalingrad relief operation (Winter Storm, 1942)
- Kharkov counteroffensive (1943)
- Kursk defensive operations (1943)
- Army Group South retreat (1943-44)

**Post-War**:
- War crimes trial (1949) - CONVICTED
- Memoir: *Verlorene Siege* (*Lost Victories*, 1955) - Tier 7 source
- Rehabilitation in Western military circles during Cold War
- Died 1973

## Propaganda Risk Assessment

**EXTREME RISK - Category 3 (Self-Authored Memoir + War Criminal)**

**Known propaganda vectors**:
1. **Self-serving memoir** (*Lost Victories*) - blames Hitler for everything, claims military genius
2. **Clean Wehrmacht myth** - downplays/denies Holocaust involvement
3. **Strategic genius narrative** - "best general of WWII" hagiography
4. **Cold War rehabilitation** - Western military establishment needed his expertise
5. **Myth of apolitical soldier** - claims he was "just following orders"

**Critical areas requiring verification**:
- Holocaust participation (11th Army in Crimea)
- War crimes (Siege of Sevastopol, scorched earth)
- Actual vs claimed operational achievements
- Relationship with Hitler and Nazi ideology
- Memoir reliability (Pattern 5: Strategic Omission)

**Operational claims requiring verification**:

- How does von Manstein present his relationship to SS/Holocaust?

- Did 11th Army participate in Einsatzgruppen operations?
- Commissar Order compliance/enforcement?

### 3. Humanization (Pyle Collaboration)

**Human context**:
