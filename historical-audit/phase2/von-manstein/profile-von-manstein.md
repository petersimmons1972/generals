## Biographical Summary

**Full Name**: Fritz Erich Georg Eduard von Lewinski, later von Manstein (adopted name)
**Born**: November 24, 1887, Berlin, German Empire
**Died**: June 9, 1973, Irschenhausen, West Germany (age 85)

**Family**:
- Born into Prussian military aristocracy (von Lewinski family)
- Adopted by uncle Georg von Manstein (took surname)
- Son's Hans-Georg killed in action, October 1942 (age 24)

**Military Career**:
- **1906**: Commissioned, 3rd Foot Guards Regiment
- **WWI**: Served Western and Eastern Fronts, wounded twice
- **Interwar**: Reichswehr staff officer (survived Treaty of Versailles restrictions)
- **1939**: Chief of Staff to Rundstedt, proposed invasion plan for France (partially adopted)
- **1941-1942**: Commander, 11th Army (Crimean campaign, Siege of Sevastopol)
- **1942-1944**: Commander, Army Group South / Army Group Don (Stalingrad relief attempt, Kharkov counteroffensive, defensive retreats)
- **March 1944**: Dismissed by Hitler (failures in Ukraine)
- **1945**: Surrendered to British forces, May 1945

**Post-War**:
- **1949**: Hamburg war crimes trial (August 23 - December 19)
  - Convicted on 9 of 17 charges
  - Sentenced to 18 years → reduced to 12 years → released after 4 years (May 7, 1953)
- **1955**: Published memoir *Verlorene Siege* (*Lost Victories*)
- **1958**: English edition with foreword by B.H. Liddell Hart
- **1973**: Died in West Germany, rehabilitated as "brilliant strategist"
