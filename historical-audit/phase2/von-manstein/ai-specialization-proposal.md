## What Von Manstein Was Actually Good At (Evidence-Based)

### Verified Operational Successes (Require Enemy Source Cross-Verification)

#### 1. Siege of Sevastopol (October 1941 - July 1942)

**Claim** (memoir): Brilliant operational planning, coordinated air/artillery/infantry assault
**Outcome**: Sevastopol captured July 1, 1942
**Verification status**: Real victory, BUT requires Soviet sources to verify von Manstein's role vs subordinates

**What needs cross-verification**:
- Did von Manstein personally plan the assault, or did he approve subordinates' plans?
- What was the casualty ratio? (High casualties may indicate brute force, not brilliance)
- How did Soviet defenders assess the German tactics? (Enemy evaluation more reliable than self-praise)

**Preliminary assessment**: Probably competent siege operations, but degree of personal contribution unclear from Western sources alone.

---

#### 2. Kharkov Counteroffensive (February-March 1943)

**Claim** (memoir): Masterful counterattack after Stalingrad disaster, encircled Soviet forces
**Outcome**: Kharkov recaptured March 15, 1943; Soviet offensive halted
**Verification status**: Real tactical success, BUT requires Soviet operational records

**What needs cross-verification**:
- Soviet force dispositions: Were Soviets overextended, making counterattack easier?
- Casualty ratios: Was this operational brilliance or numerical superiority?
- Soviet commanders' assessments: What did Vatutin, Golikov think went wrong?

**Preliminary assessment**: Probably competent exploitation of Soviet overextension, but "masterful" claim requires enemy verification.

---

#### 3. Defensive Retreats (1943-1944)

**Claim** (memoir): Conducted "mobile defense," preserved Army Group South from encirclement
**Outcome**: Army Group South retreated from Dnieper to Carpathians with heavy but not catastrophic losses
**Verification status**: Retreat happened, but "success" depends on comparing to alternatives

**What needs cross-verification**:
- Casualty rates vs other German armies in retreat
- Soviet assessments: Did they view von Manstein's retreats as competent or standard?
- Comparison to Model, Manstein's peers: Were his retreats notably better?

**Preliminary assessment**: Probably competent retreat management, which is a real skill - but not "genius," just avoiding disasters other commanders faced.
