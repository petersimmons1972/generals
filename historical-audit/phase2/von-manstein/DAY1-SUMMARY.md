## Hamburg War Crimes Trial (1949)

**Source**: Secondary sources (Wikipedia, TIME Magazine, web searches)
**Access**: SUMMARY (full transcript paywalled at UK National Archives)

**Trial**: August 23 - December 19, 1949 (62 days)
**Charges**: 17 total (3 Poland, 14 Soviet Union)

### Verdict

**GUILTY** (9 of 17 charges):
- Execution of commissars
- POW mistreatment (deaths, using for fortifications)
- Scorched earth orders harming civilians
- Deportation of civilians for slave labor
- Execution of civilians

**NOT GUILTY** (8 of 17 charges):
- **Three charges: Extermination of Jews**
- Five other charges (not specified in accessible sources)

### Sentencing
- **Original**: 18 years in prison
- **Reduced**: 12 years (February 1950)
- **Actual served**: 4 years (released May 7, 1953)
- **Early release**: Health + pressure from Churchill, Adenauer, Liddell Hart

### Why Acquitted on Holocaust Charges?

**Tribunal reasoning** (from secondary sources):
- "Insufficient direct linkage" between Manstein's directives and specific criminal acts
- High evidentiary standard (required "smoking gun" orders)
- Soviet sources potentially politically influenced (Cold War suspicion)

**Historical analysis**:
- **Cold War politics**: 1949 = Berlin Blockade, NATO formation, West German rearmament
- **Evidentiary bar too high**: Nov 20 order + wristwatch letter = clear linkage
- **Establishment pressure**: Churchill, Liddell Hart needed Wehrmacht "expertise"
- **Legal vs historical truth**: Acquittal legally defensible, historically absurd
