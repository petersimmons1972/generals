## Propaganda Risk Assessment

**MEDIUM RISK - Category 2 (Post-War Hagiography)**

**Potential propaganda vectors**:
1. **Operation Compass mythology** - "destroyed 10 Italian divisions with 3 British"
2. **"What if" narratives** - "If O'Connor hadn't been captured, could have won North Africa in 1941"
3. **Comparison inflation** - "Better than Montgomery" claims
4. **Capture romanticization** - POW period treated as heroic rather than operational failure
5. **Underdog narrative** - Small British force vs large Italian army

**Lower risk factors**:
- Allied commander (no Clean Wehrmacht myth)
- No war crimes
- Less self-promotion than Montgomery or Patton
- Died before extensive memoir campaign
- Overshadowed by Montgomery in popular history

**Critical areas requiring verification**:
- Operation Compass actual vs claimed achievements
- Italian Army quality and morale (was victory impressive or inevitable?)
- Capture circumstances (bad luck or poor decisions?)
- Normandy performance (competent or overshadowed?)
- Comparison to peers (Montgomery, Alexander)
