## VERIFIED COMPETENCIES (85/100+ Confidence)

### 1. Mobile Armor Exploitation - EXCEPTIONAL

**Evidence**:
- **Operation Compass** (Dec 1940-Feb 1941): 500-800 miles advanced in 10 weeks
- **Beda Fomm flanking maneuver**: Encircled retreating Italian Tenth Army
  - 4th Armoured Brigade (40-50 cruisers, 80 light tanks) cut off Italian escape route
  - 20,000 prisoners, 112 tanks, 216 guns, 1,500 vehicles captured
  - "All that was left of the Italian Tenth Army" destroyed
- **Source**: Australian Official History (Tier 3), National Army Museum (Tier 5)

**Tactical characteristics**:
- Rapid exploitation of breakthrough (no pause to consolidate)
- Flanking maneuvers to cut retreat routes
- Mobile forces used to encircle slower-moving enemy
- Pursuit operations maintaining pressure

**Context limitations**:
- Open desert terrain (visibility, mobility)
- Italian forces (dispersed, low morale, poor command)
- British armor/artillery superiority over Italian tankettes
- **NOT verified against first-rate mobile opponents**

**AI specialization**: **Mobile armor encirclement and pursuit (desert terrain, 1940-41 era)**

### 2. Surprise and Deception - EXCEPTIONAL

**Evidence**:
- **Sidi Barrani** (Dec 8-11, 1940): Complete operational surprise achieved
  - Attack launched on Italian holiday (Sunday + Feast of Immaculate Conception)
  - 100-mile approach march undetected
  - Secrecy so strict "hardly a detail of the plan was put on paper"
  - "Scarcely a dozen senior commanders and staff officers knew of the plan until just before execution"
  - Italian forces caught completely unprepared (28 tanks destroyed in harbor before crews ready)
- **Source**: Mackenzie Eastern Epic (Tier 4-5 official history)

**Deception capabilities**:
- Operational security (minimal written orders)
- Timing exploitation (enemy holiday, reduced reconnaissance)
- Approach march concealment (dust storms, desert navigation)
- Dumps moved forward without detection

**Context limitations**:
- Italian reconnaissance failures (not just British deception skill)
- Desert terrain enabling concealment
- Limited Italian air reconnaissance during holiday
- **NOT verified in environments with competent enemy intelligence**

**AI specialization**: **Operational surprise in low-intelligence environments**

### 3. Concentrated Force Application - STRONG

**Evidence**:
- **Force concentration** at Sidi Barrani: 31,000 British vs 80,000 Italian (but concentrated at point of attack)
- **Mackenzie**: British achieved local superiority through concentration despite numerical inferiority overall
- **Sidi Barrani results**: "Three enemy divisions and the Maletti group had been destroyed" in 3 days, <700 British casualties

**Tactical approach**:
- Concentrate mobile forces against dispersed static defenses
- Sequential destruction of isolated garrisons
- Avoid enemy strength, exploit dispersal
- Maintain operational tempo to prevent reinforcement

**Context limitations**:
- Effective against static, dispersed defenders
- Italian forces in isolated defensive positions (Sidi Barrani, Nibeiwa, Tummar camps)
- **NOT tested against concentrated, mobile defense**

**AI specialization**: **Force concentration against dispersed static defenses**
