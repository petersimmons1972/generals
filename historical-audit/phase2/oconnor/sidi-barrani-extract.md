# Sidi Barrani Battle - Primary Source Extract

**Source**: Compton Mackenzie, *Eastern Epic Vol-I* (1951), Lines 5577-5900
**URL**: https://archive.org/stream/in.ernet.dli.2015.123497/2015.123497.Eastern-Epic-Vol-i_djvu.txt
**Date of battle**: December 8-11, 1940
**Reliability**: Tier 4-5 (Official campaign history, published 1951)

---

## FORCE STRENGTHS

**British/Commonwealth forces**:
- 31,000 men
- 120 guns
- 275 tanks (50 Infantry tanks, ~137 cruisers, rest light tanks)
- 60 armoured cars

**Italian forces (estimated)**:
- 80,000 troops
- 250 guns
- 120 tanks

**Ratio**: British outnumbered 2.6:1 in troops, 2:1 in guns, but had 2.3:1 advantage in tanks

## KEY COMMANDERS

**British**:
- Supreme: General Sir Archibald Wavell
- Corps: Lieutenant-General R.N. O'Connor (Western Desert Force)
- 4th Indian Division: General Beresford-Peirse
- 11th Indian Brigade: Brigadier Savory
- 5th Indian Brigade: Brigadier Lloyd
- 16th British Brigade: Brigadier Lomax
- 7th Armoured Division: (not named in this extract)

**Italian**:
- General Maletti (Nibeiwa camp commander) - killed in action

## BATTLE TIMELINE

### December 6, 1940
- 4th Indian Division moved from defensive positions
- 50 miles covered, concentrated at Bir Kenayis (30 miles south of Matruh)
- Undetected by Italian reconnaissance

### December 7, 1940 (Saturday)
- Troops rested 50 miles inside desert, still undetected
- O'Connor read Special Order from Wavell + Churchill message
- Troops told this was NOT an exercise

### December 8, 1940 (Sunday)
- **Italian holiday**: Sunday + Feast of Immaculate Conception
- 4th Indian Division advanced 100 miles since December 6
- By 5 PM: 15 miles SE of Nibeiwa
- Night: Royal Navy shelled Maktila (15-inch guns)
- Libyan troops fled Maktila in confusion to Sidi Barrani
- RAF + Royal Navy bombarded Sidi Barrani

### December 9, 1940 (Monday)
**Nibeiwa assault** (7:30 AM):
- 11th Brigade + 7th RTR attacked from west
- Italian tanks caught in harbor: 28 tanks destroyed before crews ready
- General Maletti killed with machine gun
- By 8:25 AM: All resistance ended
- **Result**: ~3,000 Italians killed or captured

**Tummar West assault** (1:35 PM):
- Delayed by dust storms
- 7th RTR reduced to 22 tanks
- 1st Royal Fusiliers + 3/1st Punjab Regiment
- By 4 PM: Captured
- **Result**: At least 3,000 prisoners

**Tummar East counter-attack**:
- Italian counter-attack smashed by 5:30 PM
- **Result**: 200 Italians killed, 900 prisoners
- Garrison withdrew overnight to Point 90

### December 10, 1940 (Tuesday)
**Sidi Barrani assault** (4:15 PM):
- 16th Brigade advanced, engaged from 7 AM
- Sand storm reduced visibility to 150 yards
- Attack launched by Queen's + Camerons + 7th RTR
- **Result**: Sidi Barrani captured at dusk
- 2nd Libyan Division + most of 4th Blackshirt Division destroyed

### December 11, 1940
**Point 90 Camp**:
- Commander refused surrender initially
- Heavy bombardment, then tank assault
- Garrison formed up with white flags
- **Result**: 2,000 prisoners

## CASUALTY FIGURES

### British/Commonwealth (3 days):
**Less than 700 casualties total**
- Mackenzie: "The cost had been less than 700 casualties"
- No breakdown by killed/wounded

### Italian (3 days):
**Over 20,000 prisoners** (from Sidi Barrani alone, per 4th Indian Division)
- Nibeiwa: ~3,000 killed or captured
- Tummar West: At least 3,000 prisoners
- Tummar East: 200 killed, 900 prisoners
- Point 90: 2,000 prisoners
- Additional prisoners from 7th Armoured Division pursuit of Sofafi camps

**Units destroyed**:
- 2nd Libyan Division
- Most of 4th Blackshirt Division
- 1st Libyan Division (December 11)
- Maletti Group
- **Mackenzie summary**: "Three enemy divisions and the Maletti group had been destroyed"

## KEY TACTICAL DETAILS

### Surprise achieved:
- Italian holiday (Sunday + Immaculate Conception) = no air reconnaissance
- Previous day's dust storm obscured preliminary moves
- "It was impossible that they would not soon be attacked by the Regia Aeronautica. Yet not a single enemy plane appeared."

### German correspondent prediction (proven wrong):
> "Neither of the parties can carry out a surprise attack in the Egyptian Western Desert because of the natural obstacles in the desert, and because the preparation of mechanised forces for a big offensive on a large scale cannot be concealed."

### British planning:
- Secrecy so strict "hardly a detail of the plan was put on paper"
- "Scarcely a dozen senior commanders and staff officers knew of the plan until just before it was put into execution"
- Dumps moved 30 miles forward without detection

### Italian resistance quality:
- Maletti fought personally with machine gun
- "Italian and Libyan gunners fought well"
- Field guns fired at 30 yards range but "impotent against the tanks"
- Point 90 refused initial surrender but then formed up with white flags

## WAVELL'S ASSESSMENT

From Wavell's dispatch (quoted by Mackenzie):
> "The Support Group of the 7th Armoured Division was to observe the group of enemy camps round Sofafi and prevent the enemy from these camps intervening in the battle. The remainder of the Armoured Division and the 4th Indian Division were to pass through the gap between the Sofafi camps and Nibeiwa. A brigade of the 4th Indian Division with the 7th Battalion R.T.R. (Infantry tanks) was then to attack Nibeiwa camp from the west, while the Armoured Division covered the attack and prevented any intervention from the enemy to the north."

---

## HISTORICAL SIGNIFICANCE (Mackenzie's assessment)

> "The Red Eagles had achieved something larger than that; they had set the hopes of the British Commonwealth soaring with themselves. They had handed to the people of Britain jeroboams of champagne at a time when they were growing tired of flat ale"

> "Solium, Bardia, Tobruk, Derna and Benghazi were still to come, but Sidi Barrani made them possible."

**Critical point**: 4th Indian Division withdrawn December 12 to Sudan, replaced by 6th Australian Division
- Wavell made decision December 10 during battle
- Caused "good deal of depression" among troops
- 7th Armoured Division continued westward pursuit

## QUOTES WITH ATTRIBUTION

**On surprise**: "Early in the morning of a cold grey Friday... [December 6]... Fifty miles of desert had been covered, and when they had dug slit trenches the troops turned in early to sleep. Throughout Saturday, December 7th they rested without the cover of so much as sparse low scrub yet unobserved by the enemy's reconnaissance planes."

**On Nibeiwa assault**: "The Infantry tanks caught 28 Italian tanks in harbour and knocked every one of them out before the crews were ready. Then they mounted the wall and roared down into the camp. General Maletti and his officers had just sat down to breakfast. The General rushed out of the dug-out with a machine-gun and as he opened fire on the advancing forces was shot dead with a bullet through the lung."

**On casualties**: "In three days the division had taken over 20,000 prisoners with many guns, tanks and stores. Three enemy divisions and the Maletti group had been destroyed. The cost had been less than 700 casualties."
