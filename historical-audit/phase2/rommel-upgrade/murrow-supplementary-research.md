### France 1940 Statistics - Multiple Source Cross-Reference

**Pilot claim**: 97,648 prisoners captured

**Search result findings**:

**Source 1 - The Rommel Papers** (Archive.org, PDF Coffee, Scribd):
- "Its captures amounted to 97,648 prisoners, together with 277 field guns"

**Source 2 - 88th Infantry Division Archive**:
- "97,648 prisoners, together with 277 field guns, 64 anti-tank guns"

**Source 3 - Historia Militar de INISEG (BATTISTELLI)**:
- "Panzer-Division had captured 97,648 men, 277 field guns, 64 anti-tank guns"

**Source 4 - "Panzer Commander" memoirs**:
- "According to Rommel's account, the division had taken 97,648 prisoners"

**Critical finding**: ALL sources trace back to **Rommel's own papers** (edited by Liddell Hart, 1953)

**Reliability assessment**:
- **Tier 6-7**: Self-reporting via edited memoir
- **Consistency**: Exact same number across all citations
- **Independent verification**: NOT FOUND in accessible British or Allied records
- **War diary**: Located in Bundesarchiv catalogs but not accessible online

**Conclusion**: Figure is widely cited and consistent, BUT originates from Rommel's own account. Independent verification from Allied records or unedited war diary NOT achieved. This is a Tier 6-7 source (subject's own account) presented consistently, not Tier 1-2 primary verification.

**Accuracy impact**: Pilot's claim of "VERIFIED -- Murrow statistical review" from "divisional war diary" is OVERSTATED if war diary was not directly accessed. Should be: "Reported in Rommel Papers (Liddell Hart ed. 1953) as 97,648 prisoners. Widely cited but originates from Rommel's account. Independent verification not accessible."
