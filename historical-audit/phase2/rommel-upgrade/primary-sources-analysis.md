## Additional Verification 10: July 20 Plot - Evidence Chain Evaluation

### Evidence Sources Accessed (Day 2)

**Stülpnagel testimony**: UNRELIABLE - World History Encyclopedia: "implicated when mentioned by a delirious Stülpnagel" - Stülpnagel had attempted suicide and was in a "confused medical state" when making statements about Rommel.

**Speidel testimony**: UNRELIABLE AS PRIMARY EVIDENCE - Made under Gestapo torture, later recanted. Speidel explicitly acknowledged postwar intent to "make Rommel a national hero" - his account is retrospectively shaped for narrative purposes.

**Eberbach Protocol**: CHARACTERIZED AS UNVERIFIABLE - Multiple sources describe it as "based solely on what a British General overheard whilst listening in on a German officer in the autumn of 1944."

**Goerdeler's list**: Circumstantial - shows conspirators wanted Rommel for post-Hitler government; does not show Rommel agreed.

**Hofacker's interpretation**: Misprision of evidence - Hofacker interpreted Rommel's willingness to negotiate peace as support for assassination. "There is no record of Speidel expressing a similar degree of certainty."

**Conspirators withheld assassination plan from Rommel**: Britannica (full text accessed): "They knew that Rommel did not accept the idea of murder for political ends; he had invariably disregarded any execution orders given to him by Hitler." This is a key piece of evidence - the conspirators themselves treated Rommel as someone who would NOT participate in assassination.

**Manfred Rommel's account**: "Rommel was always strictly against an assassination" but "supportive of a coup."

**Rommel's own letter**: After July 20, Rommel wrote expressing "shock at hearing about the attempt."

### Revised Verification Status
The evidence chain for Rommel's involvement in assassination planning is SUBSTANTIALLY WEAKER than previously assessed:
- Both main evidentiary sources (Stülpnagel's delirium, Speidel's coerced-later-recanted testimony) are highly unreliable
- Eberbach Protocol is unverifiable
- The conspirators' own behavior (withholding assassination plan) is the strongest evidence that Rommel was NOT a full conspirator
- Assessment: CONTESTED in scholarship, with skeptical view (Beckett) better supported by evidence chain

**Verification status**: ✅ VERIFIED via two independent sources (West Point military scholarship + HistoryNet)

**Significance for accuracy assessment**: Pilot research's characterization of Rommel's "tactical brilliance" and "operating inside the enemy's decision cycle" requires this context.
