### Alexander's Official Dispatch on Alam el Halfa (Original and 1947 Revision)

**Access level**: SUMMARY (indirect, via 1962 NYT article metadata from SearXNG)
**Tier**: 1
**Source**: New York Times, 1962 - "Alexander Says He Revised '42 Battle Report; Change Stirs an Uproar"
**What was accessed**: Title and brief description only (NYT article paywalled)
**Evidence extracted**: Alexander admitted revising his official dispatch about the 1942 Battle of Alam el Halfa; the "1947 revision...linked Field Marshal Montgomery with successful plans"
**Significance**: CRITICAL - Documents that the post-war official record was altered to credit Montgomery more prominently
**Limitation**: Full text of the revision and the original dispatch not accessible
**Reliability note**: NYT 1962 = credible newspaper; Alexander himself admitted the revision

## CRITICAL ACCESS GAPS

The following questions CANNOT be definitively answered from accessible sources:

1. **Did Montgomery's operational orders specify Caen as D-Day objective?** (Requires WO 285)
2. **Was Auchinleck's Alam Halfa plan identical to what Montgomery executed?** (Requires WO 201)
3. **What did Eisenhower's contemporary efficiency reports say about Montgomery?** (Requires SHAEF records)
4. **Exactly what did Alexander's original vs. revised dispatch say about Alam Halfa?** (Requires archival access)
5. **What is the full Buckley/French/Beevor/Atkinson academic assessment?** (All paywalled)

These gaps mean the research conclusions depend heavily on SearXNG-mediated summaries of secondary sources. The accuracy rating must reflect this limitation.
