## Key Verification Questions

### 1. Alamein - Brilliance or Inevitability?

**Claims to verify**:
- "Montgomery transformed 8th Army morale"
- "Destroyed Panzerarmee Afrika at Alamein"
- "Turned the tide in North Africa"

**Evidence needed**:
- Auchinleck's preparations before Montgomery arrived
- Material balance: tanks, artillery, air superiority, fuel
- ULTRA intelligence contribution
- German strength vs British strength
- Actual casualties inflicted vs claimed

**Comparison**:
- Montgomery with material superiority vs Rommel without fuel
- Fair comparison or inevitable result?

### 2. Normandy - Plan vs Reality

**Claims to verify**:
- "Drawing German armor to Caen was the plan all along"
- "Caen delays were intentional to help Americans"
- "Goodwood was a success"

**Evidence needed**:
- Pre-D-Day plans: What did they actually say about Caen timing?
- Contemporary assessments during June-July 1944
- Bradley's views at the time (not post-war feuds)
- Eisenhower's patience vs impatience
- Statistical success: ground gained, casualties, German divisions destroyed

### 3. Market Garden - Responsibility

**Claims to verify**:
- "Plan was sound, execution failed"
- "Intelligence warnings were unclear"
- "A bridge too far" - whose fault?

**Evidence needed**:
- Dutch resistance warnings (documented? ignored?)
- Intelligence assessments of German strength
- Who approved the plan? (Eisenhower? Montgomery?)
- Post-operation responsibility acceptance

### 4. Personality vs Competence

**Claims to verify**:
- "Beloved by troops, insufferable to peers"
- "Brilliant tactician, poor diplomat"
- "Better general than Eisenhower/Bradley"

**Evidence needed**:
- Subordinate officers: how many sacked? Why?
- Peer assessments (contemporary, not post-war memoirs)
- Troop morale data vs other commanders
- Statistical performance vs American generals (ground gained per casualty, etc.)
