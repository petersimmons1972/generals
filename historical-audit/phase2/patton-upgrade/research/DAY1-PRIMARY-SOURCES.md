## Primary Sources Successfully Accessed

### 1. Patton War Diaries (1910-1945)
**Source**: Library of Congress Manuscript Division
**URL**: https://www.loc.gov/collections/george-s-patton-diaries/
**Access**: Digitized, crowdsourced transcription available
**Coverage**: Complete diaries through March 24, 1945 (originals); annotated transcripts through Dec 3, 1945

**Key Verified Quotes**:
- **August 3, 1943** (Kuhl slapping): "I gave him the devil, slapped his face with my gloves and kicked him out of the hospital. Companies should deal with such men and if they shirk their duty they should be tried for cowardice and shot."
- **August 6, 1943** (shell shock recognition): "saw two men completely out from shell shock. One kept going through the motions of crawling. The doctor told me they were going to give them an injection to put them to sleep and that probably they would wake up alright."
- **August 10, 1943** (Bennett slapping): "I may have saved his soul, if he had one."

**Status**: VERIFIED - Pilot research slapping incident details confirmed

### 2. Eisenhower Investigation Report
**Source**: Eisenhower Presidential Library via TimeGhost community
**Date**: November 26, 1943
**URL**: https://community.timeghost.tv/t/report-by-gen-eisenhower-on-the-patton-incident-11-26-43/9663

### 3. Army War College Scholarly Article
**Source**: Parameters (US Army War College Quarterly), Vol 49, No 3, Autumn 2019
**Title**: "Slap Heard around the World: George Patton and Shell Shock"
**Author**: Alexander G. Lovelace
**Pages**: 12 pages (pp. 80-91)
**URL**: https://press.armywarcollege.edu/cgi/viewcontent.cgi?article=2776&context=parameters

**Primary Sources Cited Within Article**:
- Patton diary entries (Library of Congress GSPP collection)
- Medical officer reports (Leaver, Currier) from National Archives
- Eisenhower correspondence (Presidential Library PPPF collection)
- Contemporary officer accounts (Lucas, Bradley, Hughes)

**Critical Finding - Shell Shock Nuance**:
> "a search of the general's extensive diaries, correspondence, and writings cannot produce a single reliable statement from Patton claiming shell shock was not an authentic medical condition."

Lovelace documents:
- Patton ordered Shell Shock in France 1914-1918 by Charles Myers on March 19, 1941
- August 6 diary shows Patton recognized two soldiers as genuinely shell-shocked
- December 30, 1944 letter to Hughes: directed medical evaluation and leave for combat-exhausted officers
- Apology statements: "always regarded cases of 'shell shock' as being most tragic"

**Conclusion**: Patton believed shell shock existed but adhered to WWI definition requiring complete incapacitation. Kuhl and Bennett were coherent, leading Patton to view them as malingerers, not shell-shocked.

**Status**: MAJOR NUANCE - Pilot research oversimplified this as "didn't believe shell shock existed"

### 2. J. Furman Daniel III - Patton: Battling with History (2020)
**Access**: Law & Liberty podcast/article summary
**Key Findings**:
- Patton raised with narrative of "continuous line" of warrior ancestors
- "Manipulating the historical record so that he appeared even greater than he was"

- Patton's actual voice was "high and squeaky" (not Scott's gravelly performance)
- Dual nature: genuinely talented AND meticulous self-image craftsman

**Status**: VERIFIED - Confirms pilot research on self-mythologizing pattern
