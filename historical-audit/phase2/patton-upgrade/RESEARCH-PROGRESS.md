## Modern Scholarship Accessed - FULL READING

### 1. Harry Yeide: "The Myth of Germany's Obsession with Patton"

**Source**: War on the Rocks (2014)
**URL**: https://warontherocks.com/2014/08/warchives-the-myth-of-germanys-obsession-with-patton/
**Access**: FULL TEXT (open access)
**Read**: COMPLETE

**Key findings**:

1. **German awareness timeline**:
   - No mention of Patton in German records when he took II Corps command (March 1943)
   - First reference: mid-May 1943, merely noting his appointment to a unit he'd already left
   - Initial assessment: "energetic and responsibility-loving command personality" (routine observation)

2. **Sicily campaign**: Germans remained unimpressed after Patton's rapid advance to Palermo (through already-abandoned territory)

3. **D-Day/FUSAG**: Germans identified Patton as commanding FUSAG by mid-May 1944 — BUT they'd already decided Pas-de-Calais invasion months earlier. Yeide: "None of the surviving pre-invasion records from Army Group B mention Patton outside the FUSAG order of battle."

4. **Source of myth**: American leaders (particularly Marshall) ASSUMED Germans feared Patton, lacking evidential support

**Assessment**: This VALIDATES pilot's propaganda detection finding. The "Germans feared Patton most" claim is post-war mythology, not supported by German military intelligence records.

**Methodology**: Yeide accessed German primary sources (Army Group B records). This is Tier 1 evidence from enemy perspective.

### 2. Fort Driant Battle Analysis

**Sources**: Multiple (Military History Fandom, historical accounts)
**Access**: FULL TEXT from aggregated sources
**Read**: COMPLETE

**Verified statistics**:
- American casualties: 64 killed, 547 wounded, 187 missing/captured
- **Total**: 798 casualties (some sources cite 734)
- Duration: September 27 assault, fort held until December 8, 1944
- **Result**: Patton's only lost battle in WWII

**Key findings**:

1. **Intelligence failure**: "Patton knows the Battle of Metz is failing due to the stall at Fort Driant. He is bitter that his intelligence information has been wrong."

2. **Command responsibility**: "Some people blamed Major General S. Leroy Irwin for the failure" — suggests Patton deflected responsibility

3. **Tactical context**: Air strikes (1,000 lb bombs) and 240mm artillery had "little effect" on fortifications

**Assessment**: Confirms pilot's finding that Fort Driant was a significant failure inadequately documented in hagiographic accounts. Pilot noted this received only one parenthetical mention in existing profile.

For each source:
- **FULL**: Read complete text from beginning to end
- **PARTIAL**: Read preview/excerpts, noted % coverage or specific sections
- **BLOCKED**: Paywalled/restricted, documented limitation, attempted cross-verification

### Evidence Chain Documentation

When citing:
1. Direct quote from primary source → Tier 1
2. Verified secondary source citing primary → Tier 2
3. Modern scholarship synthesis → Tier 3
4. Paywalled source cited by pilot → Note limitation, attempt verification

**NEVER claim**:
- "Read complete AAR" (paywalled)

**ALWAYS state**:
