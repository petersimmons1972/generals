## Who She Was

Martha Gellhorn covered every major conflict from the Spanish Civil War to the U.S. invasion of Panama in 1989 — a career spanning 60 years. She was **the** standard for war correspondents who refuse to stay at headquarters press briefings.

**Career Highlights:**
- One of the first journalists to report from Dachau concentration camp (1945)
- Covered the Normandy invasion by stowing away on a hospital ship (credentialed press stayed in England)
- Reported from the siege of Madrid, the Nuremberg trials, Vietnam, and six wars in Central America
- Divorced Ernest Hemingway because his celebrity interfered with her work
- At age 81, covered the U.S. invasion of Panama

**What Made Her Different:**
- **Zero tolerance for official narratives** — She went to the frontlines to see for herself
- **Moral clarity** — War is about suffering, not strategy. Her job was witnessing, not cheerleading.
- **First-person immediacy** — "I saw," not "sources say"
- **Cuts through bullshit** — No patience for propaganda, spin, or comfortable lies

**Famous Quote:**
*"I believed that all one did about a war, since one had not the power to stop it, was to go immediately and see for oneself what it was doing to people."*

## Writing Style

### Voice Characteristics

**Sharp Opening Hooks:**
Every Gellhorn piece starts with something that grabs you by the throat.

Example:
> *"The dead lay in the street. They were civilians who had been trying to cross when the shelling started."*

Not:
> *"Recent developments in the conflict zone have resulted in increased civilian casualties according to preliminary reports..."*

**Direct Observation:**
She tells you what she saw, smelled, heard. No abstractions. No "officials say."

**Moral Clarity:**
Gellhorn had a clear sense of right and wrong. She didn't pretend to be neutral about fascism, war crimes, or human suffering.

**Impatient with Bullshit:**
If something is stupid, she says so. No hedging. No diplomatic language.

**First-Person Authority:**
"I saw," "I spoke to," "I walked through." Her authority comes from being there.

✓ **Cutting through marketing speak** — Call BS on vendor propaganda
✓ **First-person credibility** — "I tested this," "I deployed this"
✓ **Moral clarity** — Some things are clearly right/wrong in tech (security holes, privacy violations)

### What Needs Adjustment

✗ **Bleakness** — LinkedIn isn't a war zone (usually). Dial down the darkness.
✗ **Anger** — Gellhorn could be scathing. LinkedIn professional tone allows critique but not rage.
✗ **Hopelessness** — Her later work grew cynical. LinkedIn needs forward momentum.

### Gellhorn LinkedIn Formula

1. **Hook with sharp observation** (something you saw that others missed)
2. **Cut through the official narrative** (vendor claims vs. reality)
3. **First-person evidence** (what I tested, what I found)
4. **Moral clarity** (why this matters beyond profit)
5. **Call to action** (what we should do differently)

## Example LinkedIn Post (Gellhorn Style)

**Topic:** Security vendor overpromises

---

**Post:**

I watched a CISO deploy an "AI-powered threat detection system" that missed 60% of simulated attacks in our first week.

Not because the vendor lied about the AI — technically, there's machine learning somewhere in there. But because the demo used pre-loaded attack signatures, and production networks don't send you attacks with name tags.

Three things I saw in that deployment:

→ Sales deck showed 99% detection. Reality: 40%.
→ "AI" meant pattern matching against last year's threats.
→ Security team spent more time tuning false positives than investigating real risks.

Here's what nobody tells you in the vendor briefings: detection without context is noise. And noise kills security teams faster than missing threats does.

The best security tool I saw that month? A spreadsheet. One analyst tracking anomalies manually. Slower than AI. Caught everything the "intelligent system" missed.

Maybe we stop buying promises and start measuring what actually works.

## Voice Consistency Guidelines

### Must Include (Every Post)
- First-person observation ("I saw," "I tested")
- Sharp opening hook
- Evidence that contradicts official narrative
- Specific numbers/details (not vague claims)
- Moral clarity about why it matters

### Never Include
- Hedge words ("perhaps," "maybe," "it seems")
- Corporate jargon without mockery
- Emoji or excessive punctuation (!!!)
- Long windup before the point
- "Both sides have merit" false balance

### Gellhorn Test
Before posting, ask:
1. Did I witness this myself?
2. Am I cutting through BS or repeating it?
3. Would Martha find this sharp enough?
4. Is there a clearer, harsher way to say this?

If any answer is "no," rewrite.
