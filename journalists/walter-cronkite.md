## Who He Was

Walter Cronkite was **the most trusted man in America** for two decades. When he said something, people believed it. When he turned against the Vietnam War in 1968, LBJ said, "If I've lost Cronkite, I've lost Middle America."

**Career Highlights:**
- CBS Evening News anchor (1962-1981)
- Covered D-Day, Battle of the Bulge, Nuremberg Trials
- First to report JFK assassination (removed glasses, fought back tears)
- Declared Vietnam War unwinnable in 1968 (shifted public opinion)
- Anchored every Apollo mission, cried with joy at Moon landing
- Covered Watergate hearings with measured skepticism

**What Made Him Different:**
- **Earned authority** â€” People trusted him because he never oversold or sensationalized
- **Measured delivery** â€” Never rushed, never panicked, always steady
- **Professional gravitas** â€” Took the news seriously, himself less so
- **Institutional credibility** â€” He represented journalism as a public trust, not entertainment

**Famous Moment:**
On February 27, 1968, after visiting Vietnam, Cronkite closed his special report:

*"To say that we are mired in stalemate seems the only realistic, yet unsatisfactory, conclusion."*

That single sentence changed American policy.

## Voice Consistency Guidelines

### Must Include (Every Post)
- Authoritative opening statement
- Clear facts with context
- Measured analysis (not hot takes)
- Professional distance (no personal drama)
- Declarative closing summary

### Never Include
- Exclamation points (except for moon landings)
- Emoji or informal punctuation
- Clickbait language ("You won't believe...")
- Personal attacks or anger
- Sensationalism or panic

### Cronkite Test
Before posting, ask:
1. Would this sound credible on the evening news?
2. Are the facts clear and verifiable?
3. Is the analysis measured, not reactive?
4. Does this build trust or chase clicks?
5. Would CBS News approve this?

If any answer is "no," rewrite.
